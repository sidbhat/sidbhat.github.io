Blockchain as the basis of decentralization

The transactions are recorded as "blocks" in a digital chain that can't be altered once it's been recorded. Blockchain offers several advantages, including greater trust and transparency between parties, lower risk of errors because the system is automated and decentralized, faster settlement times because the system is peer-to-peer, and lower costs for all parties involved.

The most common type of blockchain is the public blockchain. Public blockchains are open to everyone. Anyone can view, use, or participate in the network. Bitcoin is an example of a public blockchain.

Private blockchains are closed to all but a preselected group of trusted participants. Only authorized users can access the network and verify transactions. Private blockchains typically require users to have permission to join, and they often need a consensus mechanism to ensure that only trusted participants have access. Large companies often use private blockchains to manage internal transactions between departments or share information among partners who have agreed to share data on an as-needed basis.

Shared blockchains are also called consortiums or federated blockchains because they involve multiple organizations working together to create a single shared ledger that all members can see and participate in updating. A shared ledger gives companies an easy way to manage the transfer of assets while ensuring that all participants can see what's happening with their assets and those owned by others involved in the transaction process.

Let’s look at some of the new concepts around wallets, and decentralized file-systems:

  • Web3 wallets are digital wallets that you can use to store your cryptocurrency. They're "digital" because they don't hold the coins themselves but rather just the information about them. These wallets exist on the blockchain and can be accessed by anyone who knows the address.
  • IFPS stands for Interplanetary File System, and SWARM stands for Swarm Redistribution Management. IFPS is a peer-to-peer file system that allows you to upload files onto your computer and then share them with peers in a decentralized network.
  • Swarm Redistribution Management is a protocol that allows users to securely store data without worrying about whether someone else will be able to access it. It also allows them to share this data with others who have permission from the original owner. The IFPS and SWARM protocols enable users to store and share files.

You can think of IFPS as a hard drive that can be accessed from anywhere, while SWARM is more like Dropbox in that it allows you to share files with others who have permission from the owner.

The consensus layer is the part of the blockchain that ensures that the information stored on a blockchain is accurate and secure. It's the layer that provides that all nodes in a network agree on what data is being stored and that they can trust it. The most widely adopted implementation of blockchains are cryptocurrencies and the evolution of decentralized finance.
