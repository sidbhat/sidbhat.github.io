Future of work

Web3 Technologies will impact enterprise solutions in a big way, including:

  • Teams working across organization boundaries in dynamic teams and getting paid in cryptos and tokens.
  • Users will own their digital identity, and there is a push toward centralized identity providers.
  • Governance, risk, and compliance will have to be re-evaluated as transactions and user identities work across centralized and decentralized networks.
  • De-Fi challenges how banks operate, transfer money, manage their funds, and pay their shareholders.
  • Retail experiences will evolve as metaverses and AR/MR becomes mainstream.
  • Advertisement-based monetization models will evolve as people have more control over their identities.
  • Disintermediation will lead broker-based businesses to evaluate their new value proposition: insurance agents, realtors, car salespeople, money managers, and others.
  • Decentralized social networks will protect free speech and prevent censorship.
  • Push toward on-chain functionality & voting rights – contracts, surveys, polls, and voting.
  • Recruitment: helps recruiters find qualified and reliable applicants, as well as engage with potential hires with personalized job recommendations based on their skill set. Blockchain can store qualifications on a distributed ledger where multiple parties can access but not alter them. This means CVs could be made redundant because companies will have public access to all education certificates, allowing them to assess potential candidates automatically.
  • Payroll: transactions on the blockchain can be made more secure and transparent, including salaries and taxes. The future of payroll will also include real-time and on-demand payments in multiple currencies, including fiat and cryptos.
  • Employee verification: Verifies job applicants' identity by checking their records against educational and work history stored on the blockchain.
  • Employee safety: ensures safe working conditions for employees worldwide, especially in high-risk industries like manufacturing and construction.
  • NFTs: for employees' digital assets, such as resumes, portfolios have become very popular for gathering the right candidate for a position. A non-fungible token (NFT) is a unit of data on a blockchain representing something unique. The unique nature does not make it interchangeable with other tokens of the same type. NFTs are used mainly for two purposes - to record ownership and represent proof of ownership.
  • Metaverse-based NFTs will be used to create and manage digital twins of customers. These virtual representations of individuals can be linked to their real-world identity using biometrics or other methods, like facial recognition software.
  • The digital twin can then be used for targeted marketing campaigns, which are often more effective than traditional advertising methods because they allow companies to interact with individuals on a personal level rather than just targeting groups of people based on demographics or location.
  • Loyalty-based NFTs are digital assets earned by buying a company's products over time. Each purchase earns you more tokens and allows you to access higher-tier products at discounted rates compared to what non-members would pay.
    • For example, a company could offer its members exclusive products and services and special sales. The more they buy from the company, the more tokens they earn. This way, loyalty-based NFTs are similar to rewards programs offered by airlines or credit cards where people receive points for purchases and can redeem them later for free travel or items like gift cards.
  • The first is cross-enterprise supply chains: companies often use multiple suppliers who provide them with goods or services, but these suppliers aren't always as reliable as they need to be. Web3 helps companies connect directly to work together on projects without relying on third parties or intermediaries.
  • The second is carbon data networks: carbon emissions can be incredibly harmful to the environment and the people who live near areas where they're produced—but there's no natural way to track them accurately. Web3 helps create an ecosystem where this information can be gathered and shared freely so everyone knows their impact (and how best to reduce it).
