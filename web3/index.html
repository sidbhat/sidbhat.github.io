The Future Of Work with Web 3.0

The future of work is changing. We're moving from where we rely on large, centralized organizations to provide us with everything we need to one where individuals are empowered to do so themselves. The Internet was designed to be a decentralized, open network; let's use it that way.

Organizations and their data and ideas currently dominate the Internet. Monopoly, censorship, and control are all problems that result from this approach. Content ownership has also become a significant issue; service providers own everything a content creator says and posts.

The current Internet, concerning news vs. opinions, is in a state of crisis. A handful of companies control almost all the information on the web, using this control to manipulate people and spread fake news. The data we create online is valuable; it should belong to us, not just our service providers or social media networks.

And finally, hacking remains an ever-present threat that can endanger lives or entire economies at any moment—and there's no way for anyone but those responsible for securing our data to know what exactly is going wrong until it's too late.

This expert paper is organized to explain the core concepts of Web3 including metaverses, blockchain, NFTs and DAOs and then does an art of the possible narrative on how enterprise apps will evolve from their current state to support the future of work.

Let’s start with the portal to the future of Web3 work - Metaverse!
